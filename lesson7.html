üîÑ Exercise 1: Basic Concession Transformations

Rewrite sentence B so that it means the same as sentence A using the given concession words:

Item 1

A: Although youths are aware of the dangers, they take risks.

B: Despite

Item 2

A: Despite the fact that safety measures within consumerism are vital, they often vary based on geographical location.

B: In spite of

Item 3

A: Despite the fact that the advertisement was persuasive, it lacked crucial health and safety information.

B: In spite of

Item 4

A: Even though diabetes management requires vigilance, consumer access to healthier options can assist.

B: Despite

Item 5

A: In spite of safety precautions' mitigation of risks, consumer behavior influences overall outcomes.

B: Although

Item 6

A: Though the advertisement was enticing, it didn't address potential health and safety implications.

B: Despite

üîó Exercise 2: Sentence Combining with Concessions

Example: Obesity rates are alarming; however, consumer awareness campaigns are making strides.

Combine or rewrite the sentences using the given concession words:

Item 1

Combine: Safety concerns persist. Consumer demand for safer products is gradually influencing industries. (Despite)

Item 2

Combine: Consumer education on preventive measures is evolving. Diabetes prevalence is rising. (In spite of)

Item 3

Combine: Advertisement campaigns oversell benefits. Advertisement campaigns understate potential safety risks.

Item 4

Combine: Safety guidelines vary. Consumer responsibility remains consistent.

üéØ Exercise 3: From "However/But" to Concessions

Pattern: Sentence (1) + However/but + sentence (2) ‚Üí Although + sentence (1), sentence (2)

Example: The British came from different origins, but they succeeded to build a flourishing society ‚Üí In spite of coming from different origins, the British succeeded to build a flourishing society

Rewrite sentence B so that it means the same as sentence A:

Item 1

A: Safety regulations have improved; however, consumerism's pace often challenges these advancements.

B: Despite

Item 2

A: The advertisement highlighted health benefits, but overlooked potential safety concerns.

B: In spite of

Item 3

A: Diabetes rates have surged; however, consumer choices also impact this trend.

B: Although

Item 4

A: Advertisement tactics often prioritize sales; but safety information should be paramount.

B: Even though

Item 5

A: Diabetes management requires discipline; however, consumer access to healthier options varies.

B: Though

Item 6

A: Safety regulations differ by place; however, consumer advocacy maintains its global relevance.

B: Despite
