üìã Purpose Expression Patterns:

  • So that + subject + modal verb (can/could/will/would/may/might)
  • In order that + subject + modal verb (formal)
  • So as to + infinitive (same subject)
  • In order to + infinitive (same subject)
  • To + infinitive (same subject, most common)

Group 1: Study hard + You succeed in the exam

Item 1

Combine: Study hard. You succeed in the exam. (so that)

Item 2

Combine: Study hard. You succeed in the exam. (so as to)

Group 2: BAC students + homework + attain exams

Item 3

Combine: All the BAC students did their homework. They attain their exams. (in order that)

Item 4

Combine: All the BAC students did their homework. They attain their exams. (in order to)

Group 3: Talk to teacher + understand lessons

Item 5

Combine: They talked to the teacher. They understand the lessons. (so as to)

Item 6

Combine: They talked to the teacher. They understand the lessons. (so that)

Item 7

Combine: They talked to the teacher. They understand the lessons. (in order that)

Item 8

Combine: They talked to the teacher. They understand the lessons. (to)

Group 4: Read books + win competition

Item 9

Combine: They read 40 books. They win the competition. (so as to)

Item 10

Combine: They read 40 books. They win the competition. (so that)
